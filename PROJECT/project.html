Billie Eilish is an American singer-songwriter who rose to fame in the music industry at a very young age. Born on December 18, 2001, in Los Angeles, California, as Billie Eilish Pirate Baird O'Connell, she gained significant attention with her debut single "Ocean Eyes" in 2015. The song was initially written and produced by her brother Finneas O'Connell for his band, but Billie's version quickly went viral on various platforms like SoundCloud.

  • Early Beginnings: Billie Eilish's interest in music started at a young age. She began writing and recording her own songs with her brother, Finneas O'Connell, who is also a musician and songwriter.

  • "Ocean Eyes" (2015): Eilish gained significant attention with her debut single "Ocean Eyes," which was initially written by Finneas for his band. The song was uploaded to SoundCloud and quickly went viral, launching Billie's career.

  • Breakthrough Album - "When We All Fall Asleep, Where Do We Go?" (2019): In March 2019, Billie Eilish released her debut studio album, "When We All Fall Asleep, Where Do We Go?" The album received widespread critical acclaim and commercial success, debuting atop the charts in multiple countries. It showcased Eilish's unique sound, blending elements of pop, electropop, and alternative music.
